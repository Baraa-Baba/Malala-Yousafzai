I tell my story not because it is unique, but because it is the story of many girls. One child, one teacher, one pen, and one book can change the world. Some people only ask others to do something. I believe that, why should I wait for someone else? Why dont I take a step and move forward? about her

Pakistani activist for female education and a Nobel Peace Prize laureate. She is also the world's youngest Nobel Prize laureate, and second Pakistani to ever receive a Nobel Prize.

born July 12, 1997, Mingora, Swat valley, Pakistan

Welcoming a baby girl is not always cause for celebration in Pakistan but her father, Ziauddin Yousafzai, was determined to give her every opportunity a boy would have.

taliban took control of our town in Swat Valley

she loved school and her father -Ziauddin Yousafzai- ran a girls school in their village.
Taliban took control between 2007 and 2009, schools were destroyed and girls banned from education. also these extremists banned many things like owning a television and playing music

she got shot when she was 15

In October 2012, a member of the Pakistani Taliban boarded my school bus and shot one bullet into my left temple. she was shot because of for her campaign for the education of girls

awarded the nobel prize at 17

Yousafzai was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize as a 17-year-old in 2014, sharing the award with Kailash Satyarthi, a children's rights activist from India.

she Graduated from Oxford University

In 2020, she Graduated from Oxford University earning her degree in Philosophy, Politics and Economics
